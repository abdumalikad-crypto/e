01. Heritage & Timeline

The Birth and Evolution of an Icon

Explore the timeline of the world's most enduring sports car family, maintaining its core design philosophy since 1963.

1963

02. The First Generation (Original 911)

Designed by Ferdinand Alexander Porsche, the first prototype debuted at the Frankfurt Motor Show. Originally named the 901, it was quickly renamed to 911 due to a trademark dispute with Peugeot, sporting an air-cooled engine with 130 hp.

1974

03. G-Series and the Turbo Era

Porsche introduced forced induction to production sports cars. The legendary "930 Turbo" shook the automotive world with its wide rear wheel arches and iconic prominent "whale tail" rear spoiler.

1988

04. Type 964 & All-Wheel Drive

A massive leap forward where 85% of the 911 components were completely re-engineered. The Carrera 4 model introduced all-wheel drive and integrated modern safety features like airbags and ABS brakes.

1997

05. Type 996 & Water-Cooled Revolution

The most radical structural change in 911 history occurred: abandoning the traditional air-cooled boxer engine. To satisfy emission regulations and boost power, a water-cooling engine system was adopted.

2011

06. Type 991 — Aluminum Architecture

An innovative chassis build using extensive lightweight aluminum-steel composite structures. A longer wheelbase significantly improved driving stability at high-speed cornering scenarios.

Today

07. Type 992 — Digital Masterpiece

The most sophisticated and capable 911 to date. Featuring a wider body, high-end digital connectivity, standard Night Vision cameras, and an intelligent "Wet Mode" acoustic system for maximum rain safety.
